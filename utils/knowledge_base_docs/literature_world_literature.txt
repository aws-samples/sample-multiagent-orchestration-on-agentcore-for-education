Literature - World Literature

World literature encompasses literary works from diverse cultures, languages, and historical periods. Ancient epics like Gilgamesh, Iliad, and Ramayana establish foundational narrative patterns. Medieval literature includes Dante's Divine Comedy and Arthurian romances. Modern world literature features authors like Kafka, García Márquez, and Achebe exploring universal themes through cultural specificity. Translation challenges include preserving meaning, style, and cultural context. Comparative literature examines cross-cultural influences, shared themes, and different literary traditions. Globalization increases access to diverse voices and perspectives.
