History - Cold War Era

The Cold War (1947-1991) was a prolonged period of geopolitical tension between the United States and Soviet Union, characterized by ideological conflict, nuclear arms race, and proxy wars.

Key Cold War elements:
- Ideological divide: Capitalism vs. Communism
- Nuclear deterrence: Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD)
- Space race: Competition for technological supremacy
- Proxy wars: Korea, Vietnam, Afghanistan conflicts
- Berlin Wall: Symbol of divided Europe (1961-1989)

The Cold War ended with Soviet Union collapse in 1991, leaving the United States as the sole global superpower and reshaping international relations.
