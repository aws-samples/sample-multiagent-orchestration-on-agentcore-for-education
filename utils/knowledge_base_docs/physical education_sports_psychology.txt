Physical Education - Sports Psychology

Sports psychology examines mental factors affecting athletic performance and exercise participation. Motivation theories explain intrinsic and extrinsic factors driving participation. Goal setting uses specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound objectives. Anxiety and arousal affect performance through inverted-U relationships. Concentration and attention focus on relevant cues while filtering distractions. Confidence and self-efficacy influence effort and persistence. Team dynamics involve communication, leadership, and cohesion. Mental training techniques include visualization, relaxation, and positive self-talk improving performance and enjoyment.
