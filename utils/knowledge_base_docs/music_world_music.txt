Music - World Music Traditions

World music encompasses diverse musical traditions reflecting cultural values, histories, and social functions. African music emphasizes rhythm, call-and-response, and polyrhythmic patterns. Asian traditions include Indian ragas, Chinese pentatonic scales, and Japanese gagaku court music. European folk music varies by region with distinct instruments and dance forms. Latin American music blends indigenous, African, and European influences. Middle Eastern music uses microtonal scales and ornamental techniques. Each tradition serves social functions including religious ceremonies, work songs, and entertainment. Globalization creates fusion styles while preserving traditional practices.
