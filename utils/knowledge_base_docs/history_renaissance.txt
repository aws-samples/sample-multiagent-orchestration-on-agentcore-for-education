History - Renaissance Period

The Renaissance (14th-17th centuries) marked a cultural rebirth in Europe, emphasizing humanism, art, science, and learning. It bridged medieval and modern periods through intellectual and artistic achievements.

Renaissance characteristics:
- Humanism: Focus on human potential and achievements
- Scientific method: Empirical observation and experimentation
- Artistic innovation: Perspective, realism, classical themes
- Literature: Vernacular languages, printing press spread
- Exploration: Geographic discoveries, trade expansion
- Religious reform: Protestant Reformation challenged Catholic Church

Key figures include Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Shakespeare, Galileo, and Machiavelli. The Renaissance established foundations for modern science, art, literature, and political thought.
