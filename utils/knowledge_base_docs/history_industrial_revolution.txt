History - Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution (1760-1840) transformed society from agricultural to manufacturing-based economies. Steam power, mechanization, and factory systems revolutionized production and social structures.

Key innovations and changes:
- Steam engine: Powered factories, trains, and ships
- Textile machinery: Mechanized cloth production
- Iron and steel production: Enabled construction and transportation
- Railroad networks: Connected markets and resources
- Factory system: Centralized production and wage labor
- Urbanization: Population shift from rural to industrial cities

The Industrial Revolution increased productivity, created new social classes, and established the foundation for modern capitalism and industrial society worldwide.
