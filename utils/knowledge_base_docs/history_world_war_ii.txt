History - World War II

World War II (1939-1945) was the deadliest conflict in human history, involving most nations and resulting in 70-85 million deaths. It reshaped global politics and established the modern international order.

Major events and consequences:
- Axis Powers: Germany, Italy, Japan sought territorial expansion
- Allied Powers: Britain, Soviet Union, United States, China opposed Axis
- Holocaust: Systematic genocide of six million Jews and others
- Pacific Theater: Pearl Harbor attack, island-hopping campaigns, atomic bombs
- European Theater: Blitzkrieg, Battle of Britain, D-Day invasion
- War's end: Germany surrendered May 1945, Japan surrendered August 1945

WWII led to the United Nations formation, Cold War beginning, decolonization, and establishment of human rights principles.
