English - Rhetoric and Composition

Rhetoric studies persuasive communication through ethos (credibility), pathos (emotion), and logos (logic). Composition processes include prewriting, drafting, revising, and editing stages. Essay structures organize ideas through introduction, body paragraphs, and conclusion. Thesis statements present main arguments supported by evidence and analysis. Audience awareness shapes tone, style, and content choices. Research writing incorporates sources through quotation, paraphrase, and summary with proper citation. Revision focuses on organization, clarity, and coherence while editing addresses grammar, mechanics, and style.
