History - Ancient Civilizations

Ancient civilizations laid the foundations for modern society through innovations in government, law, technology, and culture. These early societies developed complex social structures and lasting contributions.

Major ancient civilizations:
- Mesopotamia: First cities, cuneiform writing, Code of Hammurabi
- Ancient Egypt: Pyramids, hieroglyphics, mummification, pharaohs
- Ancient Greece: Democracy, philosophy, theater, Olympic Games
- Roman Empire: Law system, engineering, military organization
- Ancient China: Paper, gunpowder, compass, Great Wall
- Indus Valley: Urban planning, drainage systems, standardized weights

These civilizations created writing systems, legal codes, architectural marvels, and philosophical traditions that continue influencing modern society and governance.
