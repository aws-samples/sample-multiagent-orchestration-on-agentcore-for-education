Literature - Dramatic Literature

Dramatic literature encompasses plays written for theatrical performance, combining dialogue, action, and stagecraft. Classical drama includes Greek tragedies and comedies establishing dramatic conventions. Shakespearean drama features complex characters, poetic language, and universal themes. Modern drama explores psychological realism, social issues, and experimental forms. Dramatic elements include plot structure, character development, dialogue, and stage directions. Performance aspects consider acting, directing, set design, and audience interaction. Contemporary theater incorporates multimedia, immersive experiences, and diverse cultural perspectives challenging traditional dramatic boundaries.
